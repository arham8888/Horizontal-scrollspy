First heading

Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to the capability of a computer or robot to perform tasks that typically require human intelligence.

Second heading

Machine learning (ML) is a branch of artificial intelligence (AI) that involves the development and application of algorithms that enable computers to learn from and make predictions based on data.

Third heading

AI hallucinations are incorrect or misleading results that AI models generate. These errors can be caused by a variety of factors, including insufficient training data, incorrect assumptions made by the model, or biases in the data used to train the model.

Fourth heading

AI hallucinations refer to inaccuracies generated by AI models, often resulting in false or nonsensical outputs. Key types include: Incorrect Predictions: When the AI wrongly predicts an event that is unlikely to occur. False Positives: Instances where the AI identifies something that does not exist. Fabricated Information: Instances where the AI generates plausible but entirely fictional statements or facts.

Fifth heading

To use AI responsibly, ensure continuous training and development for employees on safe practices. Always be transparent about how AI is used in your projects, take responsibility for the output, and design your AI models to include fairness goals utilizing representative datasets for testing and training. Remember to double-check important information related to AI use and ethics.
